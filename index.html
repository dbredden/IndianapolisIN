Indianapolis, Indiana

The most-populous city in the state of Indiana and the seat of Marion County

Indianapolis anchors the 29th largest economic region in the U.S. based primarily on the sectors of finance and insurance, manufacturing, professional and business services, education and health care, government, and wholesale trade.

Fun Facts about Indianapolis

  1. Indianapolis replaced Corydon as the state capital in 1821.
  2. Indianapolis is the largest major U.S. city that's not located on a navigable river.
  3. Crown Hill Cemetery is the third largest cemetery in the United States
  4. The Children's Museum of Indianapolis is the largst children's museum in the world.
